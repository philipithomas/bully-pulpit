- **Pinion** — a small gear that meshes with a larger gear or rack. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pinion)
- **Claxon** — an electrically operated horn
- **Quokka** — a small marsupial native to Western Australia
- **Epigraph** — a short quotation at the beginning of a book or chapter
- **Pastiche** — an artistic work in a style that imitates that of another work, artist, or period
- **Manicule** — a typographical symbol of a pointing hand, used to direct the reader's attention. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manicule) 👉🏻
- **Jig** — a device that holds a piece of work and guides the tool operating on it. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jig_(tool))
- **Pablum** — bland or insipid intellectual fare, entertainment, etc.
- **Cairn** — a mound of rough stones built as a memorial or landmark. [Three-Country Cairn](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Three-Country_Cairn)
- **Andon** — a manufacturing term for a system to notify management, maintenance, and other workers of a quality or process problem. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andon_(manufacturing))
- **Bunkobon** — a small, pocket-sized Japanese book format
- **Binnacle** — a stand or housing for a ship's compass. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Binnacle)
- **Imprimatur** — an official license to print or publish
