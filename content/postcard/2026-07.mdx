My monthly newsletter about what I am up to, which [I send in place of social media](https://www.philipithomas.com/posts/how-to-replace-social-media-with-a-personal-newsletter).

## What I did in June

- I published [Spring cleaning](/spring-cleaning), a note about pruning old projects, shutting down old software obligations, and turning [philipithomas.com](/) into the main personal software project I want to keep tending. I also mentioned a larger transition: I left [Chroma](/chroma), and I start a new role in August.
- I flew to Japan, bought a Leica M11-P, and launched [*Tsundoku*](/tsundoku), a pop-up photography newsletter that will run until 2026-07-07. I introduced the project in [Introducing Tsundoku](/introducing-tsundoku), then started posting photos from Tokyo, Kyoto, Kanazawa, and Karuizawa: [First photo](/first-photo), [Shinkansen](/shinkansen), [Downpour](/downpour), [Hi-fi](/hi-fi), [Reflecting pool](/dt-suzuki-reflecting-pool), [Vinyl](/record-bar-analog), and [Avant-garde coffee](/avant-garde-coffee) are a few favorites so far.
- I spent a satisfying amount of time around coffee, stationery, trains, and hi-fi bars. The high point was stumbling into [Lion](https://maps.app.goo.gl/QA6XCsyAgJgcn9rR7?g_st=ic), where they happened to be playing two of my favorite pieces: Dvorak's cello concerto and Kabalevsky's cello concerto.
- I visited the Walt Disney Family Museum and was delighted by the Lilly Belle, Walt Disney's 1:8-scale live steam locomotive. I also had a straight flush in a poker game, which seems worth recording somewhere.

## Things to share

- **Articles:** Blackbird Spyplane's [Fashion logos are cheesy](https://www.blackbirdspyplane.com/p/clothes-with-designer-logos-are-cheesy) makes a useful distinction: a logo from a non-clothing company can work as a souvenir, while a clothing-brand logo often makes the garment feel like advertising. Cal Newport's [Instead of taking your job, A.I. might transform it](https://www.newyorker.com/culture/open-questions/instead-of-taking-your-job-ai-might-transform-it) argues that AI resembles an intern or custom-tool maker more than a factory machine. I have also been chewing on [this NYT opinion piece](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/06/17/opinion/ai-dangerous-openai-anthropic.html?smid=nytcore-ios-share) about AI companies and doom rhetoric, Drew Austin's [I'm beginning to see the light](https://kneelingbus.substack.com/p/im-beginning-to-see-the-light), and Byrne Hobart's older polemic, [The 30-year mortgage is an intrinsically toxic product](https://byrnehobart.medium.com/the-30-year-mortgage-is-an-intrinsically-toxic-product-200c901746a).
- **Books and magazines:** [Cherry Bones](https://cherrybonesmag.com/) is a print-only coffee magazine. Issue two includes Geoffrey Moss on how, in Philadelphia and elsewhere, the bohemian bargain is nearly dead. [Eris](https://eris.press/) is an independent press I want to spend more time with, especially [Store Kongensgade 23](https://eris.press/Store-Kongensgade-23) by Soren Ulrik Thomsen. I also picked up [Mundo Mendo, Book One](https://www.mundomendo.com/books/), Luis Mendo's printed collection of illustrated stories from his first year of [Mundo Mendo](https://www.mundomendo.com/).
- **Podcasts:** Patrick McKenzie reading [Forty ways to pay for coffee in Japan](https://open.spotify.com/episode/1xsu68iBeEAzKG58BqdW0k?si=9OodMSk5RpGI17xiMa_KAw) was useful background while trying to pay for things in Japan. He is describing a real phenomenon: every register seems to support forty payment methods, while still somehow preferring cash.
- **Films/TV:** I have been watching [_Clarkson's Farm_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Clarkson%27s_Farm), Jeremy Clarkson's series about running a farm in the Cotswolds. The show is fun partly because it is a workplace comedy, and partly because farming remains legible as real work.
- **Music:** James Blake has been in rotation again, especially the baroque and harpsichord edges. I keep finding myself interested in music that sounds ornate without sounding precious.
- **Ideas:** I have been thinking about [Stolpersteine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stolperstein) and [preference falsification](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Preference_falsification): small public reminders of private lives, and the private gap between what people say and what they believe. Also, United Airlines now stocks cans of cold brew coffee, which brings me joy.
- **Objects:** I am newly amused by [tenugui](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tenugui). They are cheap, packable, and useful in a way that makes most travel accessories look overdesigned.
- **AI:** Robin Sloan's [Talkie and Claude (no, the other one)](https://www.robinsloan.com/lab/talkie/) is a good rabbit hole about language models trained only on pre-1930 text and whether they can make connections across a century of missing context.
