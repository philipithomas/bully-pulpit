I mentioned in [Spring cleaning](/spring-cleaning) that I have some downtime between jobs. I decided to fly to Japan, upgrade my camera, and spend a couple of weeks practicing photography. So, I arrived Tuesday this week and have a growing stack of photos I am proud of.

Normally, I share photos as the cover art on my posts here on the site. But I have more photos than essays right now.

This reminded me of a Japanese concept I shared in my [Postcard newsletter last August](/2025-08): [*tsundoku*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tsundoku), acquiring books and letting them pile up unread. While I definitely do that with books, this felt like the concept applying to photos.

So, today I am introducing [Tsundoku](/tsundoku), a pop-up photography newsletter from this trip.

The format is inspired by [Craig Mod's pop-up newsletters](https://craigmod.com/essays/popup_newsletters/): a temporary publication with a clear beginning and end. Tsundoku will run for about two weeks, then stop when the trip is over.

[Luis Mendo](https://www.luismendo.com/) helped me with the logo, which is set in his proprietary and private typeface Mendo Titles Bold.

Existing newsletter subscribers are *not* automatically added to *Tsundoku*. If you want to follow along, [go to the Tsundoku page](/tsundoku), where I have the first two photos up. There, you can optionally enter your email to subscribe to updates. Expect several photos per day for the next two weeks, then no emails at all afterward.

I think of Tsundoku as a project peer to Postcard and Booklet. As I [wrote](/spring-cleaning), I am trying to move away from software-as-a-service projects and toward creative work I can build for myself. Tsundoku is both: a project, and some new software to support that project.

I finished most of the infrastructure and UI for Tsundoku today on the Shinkansen from Kanazawa to Tokyo, amid [two typhoons](https://japannews.yomiuri.co.jp/society/general-news/20260627-335097/). I improved the photo-viewing web interface a lot for this project - check it out.

[Follow Tsundoku](/tsundoku).
