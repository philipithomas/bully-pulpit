I mentioned in [Spring cleaning](/spring-cleaning) that I have some downtime between jobs. I decided to fly to Japan, buy a new camera, and spend a couple of weeks practicing photography.

This is my third trip to Japan. The first was for [Almost Perfect](/almost-perfect), where I met [Luis Mendo](https://www.luismendo.com/) and his wife Yuka. I keep returning because Japan is easy to explore without a car, has great food and coffee, and lets me meet otaku for almost any niche interest. There is a culture of craft here that makes almost every rabbit hole feel worth following.

Walking around with a manual camera and no phone in my hand has changed the trip. I keep meeting people unexpectedly, and the camera makes me study my physical surroundings instead of drifting through them. The point is not to generate content or make a tour guide. It is to pay better attention to the places I am lucky enough to visit.

Normally, I share photos as cover art for essays here on the site. But after a couple of days, I already had more photos than essays.

This reminded me of a Japanese concept I shared in my [*Postcard* newsletter last August](/2025-08): [*tsundoku*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tsundoku), acquiring books and letting them pile up unread. While I definitely do that with books, this felt like the concept applied to photos.

So, today I am introducing [*Tsundoku*](/tsundoku), a pop-up photography newsletter from this trip.

The format is inspired by [Craig Mod's pop-up newsletters](https://craigmod.com/essays/popup_newsletters/): a temporary publication with a clear beginning and end. *Tsundoku* will run until 2026-07-07, then stop when the trip is over.

Luis helped me with the logo, which is set in his proprietary and private typeface Mendo Titles Bold. If you like his work, read [Mundo Mendo](https://www.mundomendo.com/), his blog.

Existing newsletter subscribers are not automatically added to *Tsundoku*. If you want to follow along, [go to the *Tsundoku* page](/tsundoku), where I have the first two photos up. There, you can optionally enter your email to subscribe to updates. Expect several photos per day until 2026-07-07, then no emails at all afterward. You can also ignore email and check the page as I add new photos.

As I wrote in [Spring cleaning](/spring-cleaning), I am trying to move away from software-as-a-service projects and toward creative work I can build for myself. *Tsundoku* is both: a photography project and some new software to support that project.

I finished most of the infrastructure and UI today on the Shinkansen from Kanazawa to Tokyo, amid [two typhoons](https://japannews.yomiuri.co.jp/society/general-news/20260627-335097/). It was my first time using the [Codex app for Mac](https://developers.openai.com/codex/app), and I was impressed. The photo viewer ended up with preloading, image-loading states, responsive layouts, and Git LFS-backed assets. That is the kind of polish I would rarely have bothered to build by hand.

I am only a couple of days into the trip, so the stack is still growing. I have photos of good food, coffee, whiskey, and toast queued up already.
