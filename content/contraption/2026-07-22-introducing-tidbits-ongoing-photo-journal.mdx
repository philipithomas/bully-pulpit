During my trip to Japan, I did the pop-up newsletter [*Tsundoku*](/introducing-tsundoku). I had a lot of fun sharing my photography there during that trip. It gave me an excuse to wake up at 4 a.m. to catch the sunrise light and forced me to analyze my photos to pick the handful that I should edit and publish. And photography has gotten me involved in a photography group back in San Francisco. As somebody who [does not use social media](/how-to-replace-social-media-with-a-personal-newsletter), I have had fun building this Instagram-like functionality into my own site in a way that works for me.

Now that *Tsundoku* has ended, I have decided to start an ongoing photography newsletter called *tidbits*. Same deal: you are not automatically subscribed. To join, go to the *tidbits* page to join the newsletter. (Or [text `SUBSCRIBE`](/text-messaging) to get photos via SMS.)

I think there is something powerful about a newsletter having a start and a stop. With photography, it allows the photos to tell a cohesive story. I intend for *tidbits* to be more of an ongoing sharing of photographs I take. But I may still do additional pop-up photography newsletters in the future for trips or events that have discrete starts and ends.
