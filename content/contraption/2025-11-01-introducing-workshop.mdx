Contraption Company began as a home for my projects like [Postcard](https://postcard.page), [Booklet](https://booklet.group), and [FRCTNL](https://frctnl.xyz). Over time, I made other experimental tools such as [QuesoGPT](/chat-with-my-dog), [a Mac Mini data center](/a-mini-data-center), [an MCP server](/mcp-and-the-future-of-ai), and a [digital junk drawer](/app-of-ones-own). Essays about these experiments have found an audience: over 100,000 people have read this site so far this year.

Through that process, I’ve realized two things. First, I enjoy crafting digital tools. Second, I like sharing what I learn along the way. I strive to maintain a high quality bar for posts on the main Contraption Company site - writing about finished work. But good projects take time, and that meant I was publishing less. For instance, [open-sourcing Postcard](/postcard-open-source) took nearly two months, during which I didn't publish a single post.

I was focusing too much on outcomes instead of the journey, and I craved a space to share half-baked ideas, sketches, and prototypes. Writing about unfinished work feels lighter. It keeps the process fun - and makes it easier to walk away from ideas that don’t deserve finishing.

I enjoy books about craft - how people actually make things - such as [What I Know About Running Coffee Shops](https://amzn.to/47F46pj), [Fäviken](https://amzn.to/47xMf3o), [Rene Redzepi's journals](https://amzn.to/4okeLgd), [Creative Selection](https://amzn.to/4p8ljyz), and [Notes from a Fellow Traveller](https://www.tannens.com/products/notes-from-a-fellow-traveller). They made me want to share my own work in the same way.

This summer, I started [_Workshop_](/workshop) as an experimental newsletter focused on work in progress: shorter posts, less refined, more in-the-weeds. I shared it with a few friends to test the idea - and found myself writing more and having fun. So I’m now “graduating” it from experiment to project - something I plan to continue indefinitely.

So far, there are a [dozen posts on _Workshop_](/workshop): on [backup plans](/backup-plans), [hacking Y Combinator](/hacking-yc-again), [email management](/my-favorite-tool-for-making-email-less-stressful), and more.

Going forward, I plan to post more often on _Workshop_ about ongoing projects, and to use this main Contraption Company list for more polished, completed work.

You can [manage your preferences](/account) to choose which newsletters you receive.

Stay tuned.
