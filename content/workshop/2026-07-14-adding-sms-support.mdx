As an experiment, I have added SMS subscription support to this website.

To subscribe, text `SUBSCRIBE` to [+1 212 347 3190](sms:+12123473190?body=SUBSCRIBE). Then, you will receive a text message about all new posts. (It is optimized for US-based numbers, but I'm consdiering expanding it to WhatsApp in the future.)

I have been [off social media for years](/how-to-replace-social-media-with-a-personal-newsletter), and have slowly been building this website into my personal homepage on the internet. Today, most people subscribe by email, and a couple follow via RSS. But, a surprising number of people have told me that they do not use email. For some, their job doesn't require it - so they don't check it often. For others, email is an overwhelming to-do list of spam and robot messages that feels too muchlike work. So, I built SMS support for them.

The system uses a dedicated Twilio number, which I [originaly set up as a voicemail](/sync-phone-numbers-and-webhooks). To send text messages, I went through the rigmarole of [A2P 10DLC registration](https://www.twilio.com/docs/messaging/compliance/a2p-10dlc/quickstart), the new-ish industry standard that US telecom carriers created in response to spam and unsolicited messages.

As a fun aside, you can text the same number with a question for the site's [AI assistant, Bell](/three-ring-binder). Bell can research my past posts to answer questions such as, "What is Philip's favorite restaurant?"

Try it out. SMS is not for everybody, but this is a fun, [open-source experiment](https://github.com/philipithomas/bully-pulpit) aligned with [my recent decision to put more creative energy into this site](/spring-cleaning).
