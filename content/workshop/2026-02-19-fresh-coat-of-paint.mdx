Recently I have been watching documentaries by [Gary Hustwit](https://www.hustwit.com/films). It started with [Rams](https://www.amazon.com/gp/video/detail/B0GGBXDVX4?&linkCode=ll2&tag=contraption-20&linkId=0be2b7f1d033ebd3b3641f1d8f12141c&language=en_US&ref_=as_li_ss_tl), about the life of Dieter Rams. Next, [Urbanized](https://www.amazon.com/Urbanized-Norman-Foster/dp/B006O5RH2Y?&linkCode=ll2&tag=contraption-20&linkId=047346d4dcc4f075fc4af8d45d86e563&language=en_US&ref_=as_li_ss_tl) about city design. Then, [Helvetica](https://www.amazon.com/s?url=search-alias%3Dinstant-video&field-keywords=helvetica&crid=2TJ6Z6E9QRDC&sprefix=helec%2Cinstant-video%2C589&linkCode=ll2&tag=contraption-20&linkId=3bceaf2f152a8e1b1ec792b76a354677&language=en_US&ref_=as_li_ss_tl) about the popular font.

As I watched _Helvetica_, I confronted the fact that Contraption Company used the Helvetica font. I saw designers talking about the beauty of Helvetica: a revolutionary, canonical font. But also that it was generic, used for everything, becoming ambient and insipid. During the documentary, I decided it was time to stop using Helvetica and refresh the Contraption website.

The recent Contraption site was influenced by modern, minimalist Norwegian websites, such as [Dekode](https://dekode.no/), [Netlife](https://www.netlife.com/), and [Snøhetta](https://www.snohetta.com/). As a non-designer, I gravitate towards minimalism because limited palettes seem safe and hard to screw up. But I was ready for something more distinctive. I was inspired by sites like [Aimé Leon Dore](https://www.aimeleondore.com/), [Soho House](https://www.sohohouse.com/), and [Noma Projects](https://nomaprojects.com/) for a refresh that is still modern but brings in more character.

![Old contraption site in Helvetica](/images/posts/fresh-coat-of-paint/localhost_2368_.png)

I started with the fonts. I explored [Berkeley Mono](https://usgraphics.com/products/berkeley-mono), which I use for coding. I liked [FF Meta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/FF_Meta). [Playtype](https://playtype.com/) in Copenhagen had some cool options. But I was ultimately enamored by the fonts from the [Klim Type Foundry](https://klim.co.nz/fonts/) in New Zealand, settling on [Söhne](https://klim.co.nz/collections/soehne/) paired with [Tiempos](https://klim.co.nz/fonts/tiempos-text/).

Over the long weekend, I [rewrote the entire theme](https://github.com/contraptionco/contraption-ghost-theme/pull/6) with the help of [Claude Code](https://claude.ai/). At first glance, the site still looks minimal. But there is more color, more interactions, updated pages like [Projects](https://contraption.co/projects/), and subtly different moods for different corners like [Workshop](/workshop). I wanted the website to feel like a maze: something you explore, with texture and surprises around each corner.

One decision I made was to remove dark mode support. Somebody recently told me that dark mode frenzy was a sign that the technology industry was bored before the rise of AI. Supporting both light and dark mode makes sites converge to the same generic [shadcn/ui](https://ui.shadcn.com/) look. Generic can be good for software tools that people use all day, like [Notion](https://notion.com). But for a more niche site, looking generic keeps you from being interesting or memorable. Without that constraint, I made the site visually interesting to explore, like the [entirely inverted 404 page](/404).

[Take a look](https://contraption.co), explore some pages, and let me know what you think. Now, back to the documentary queue.

P. S. - the [Print Edition](/print) template has been updated, too.
