Today's chat tools feel dated. It's time for a startup to rethink them.

[Notion](https://notion.so) is an example of a team firing on all cylinders to become AI-native. But, no communication tools seem to have cracked AI.

I believe there's an opportunity for a startup to take on chat. Something built from the ground-up with AI. Something that becomes "ChatGPT for teams", growing in knowledge as teams use it, and self-training AI agents for the entire team.

Imagine opening the app, clicking the universal "Compose" button to type “the app seems down”, then the AI directing you to the `oncall` channel, explaining that there’s already a thread going, and drafting a recommended message for you to post `+1 - I'm also unable to access the site.`

I'm not going to build this. But, if I were going to - here would be my wishlist:

-   A personal AI bot who can run searches for you and return results, like "find and summarizes all ideas to improve our pricing page"
-   Live proofreading of messages
-   AI image generation inline
-   Smart "to-do" list, including being able to assign to-dos to other people
-   Detecting when somebody is waiting on a response from you, and adding it to your to-do list
-   AI titles and summaries of ongoing threads
-   Detecting when discussions have reached a decision, posting the outcome in the channel, and closing the thread
-   Allow users to make their own bots, including knowledge, tools, and prompt-level controls - no [AI horseless carriage](https://koomen.dev/essays/horseless-carriages/)
    -   Marketing team can train their bot on guidelines, like "use oxford commas", and other teams can ask questions of it
    -   Engineering team can make a moderation bot, which auto-removes offtopic posts from their `oncall` channel
    -   Product team can have a bot that tags the relevant PM in every support disucssion - "Sales team is talking about a feature you work on"
    -   HR team can build a bot that auto-labels discussions in its channel, such as "Question" or "Feedback"
    -   Execs can have a bot that sends a daily summary of discussions across teams ("Here were biggest customer support issues yesterday), but can escalate high-priority discussions in real-time
-   Prompt-level control of which notifications you want from which channel
-   Full support for users without photos (showing initials instead of blob photos)
-   [No presence indicator](https://world.hey.com/jason/the-presence-prison-69608e0f/) because talking about work is not a discussion for doing work
-   Shared channels across teams
