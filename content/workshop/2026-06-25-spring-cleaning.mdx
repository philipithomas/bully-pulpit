In [Software in the AI era](https://www.philipithomas.com/software-in-the-ai-era), I wrote about how software is moving from something you *buy from others* to something you *build for yourself*. In that spirit, I took some time in recent weeks to simplify the things I work on - because ongoing commitments become obligations, with an overhead of [maintenance](https://www.philipithomas.com/maintenance).

I enjoyed my [experiments with OpenClaw](https://www.philipithomas.com/openclaw-is-my-new-coworker), but shut down the server. I did not need a personal assistant beyond the capabilities of ChatGPT, and it is a scary piece of software because of its access. But, Bell lives on as a feature on [philipithomas.com](/) that can do deep research over my posts.

In 2023, I started building [Booklet](/launching-booklet), a community forum leveraging AI to turn discussions into a readable newsletter - a concept I described in [community-powered media companies](/community-media-companies). I had launched the [FRCTNL](https://www.philipithomas.com/introducing-frctnl) community for tech freelancers as a test of the software. In the years since, FRCTNL continued to grow, but Booklet didn't have traction or paying customers beyond FRCTNL. With AI agents, I found that operating the platform and the community became a battle against AI spam. So, in an act of creative destruction - I have shut down Booklet and FRCTNL. I intend to open-source Booklet in the coming days, with some more details of how it worked. Thanks to every who tried this software and participated in the community.

I had some old websites still in an old Webflow account. Webflow is a good tool for living websites (like [anthropic.com](https://anthropic.com), which seems to use it), but for "finished" pages, such as an old wedding site, its pricing structure does not make sense. With Claude's [Chrome plugin](https://code.claude.com/docs/en/chrome), I migrated all of my old Webflow sites to pixel-perfect copies in Next.js, hosted for free on Vercel - allowing me to delete my Webflow account.

I recently [wrote about rebuilding philipithomas.com into a Next.js app with a custom Rust mailing server](https://www.philipithomas.com/software-in-the-ai-era). And, I have continued to improve on that stack. When I got access to [Claude Fable](https://www.anthropic.com/news/claude-fable-5-mythos-5) for a few days (before Anthropic paused access), I went crazy improving the site: [shipping over 120 pull requests with Fable in a single day](https://github.com/philipithomas/bully-pulpit/graphs/contributors?from=5%2F23%2F2026). I ended up killing the Rust mailing server, and moving the entire site to Vercel. While most of my projects run on [my Mac Mini](/how-to-host-web-apps-on-a-mac-mini), I found that the developer experience of Vercel was worth the premium - such as pull request previews, and their [Workflows](https://vercel.com/workflows) product. From little improvements like [better email templates](https://github.com/philipithomas/bully-pulpit/pull/59) to bigger projects like a [local embeddings-based search index](https://github.com/philipithomas/bully-pulpit/pull/49), the site has gotten much more polished. I plan to continue to treat it as the outlet for my creative tinkering. If you want to follow along, check out the [bully-pulpit repo on GitHub](https://github.com/philipithomas/bully-pulpit), which is now the single repo powering my website, subscriptions, and newsletter mailing.

Finally, in a non-software world: after years of using [Overcast](https://overcast.fm/) as my podcast player, I migrated all of my podcast subscriptions to Spotify. I have long used Spotify for music, its excellent audiobooks feature prompted me to centralize my audio content into a single app.

With my mind more clear, I can now concentrate on some new adventures. More details soon.
